For educators

Use the lab, keep the reasoning.

The site is well suited to demonstrations, exploratory homework, and follow-up discussion because the interaction can be paired with seed values, state measurements, and verification-friendly exports.

Good classroom uses
  • Live demonstration. Show the phenomenon before introducing the formal model.
  • Structured exploration. Ask students to compare parameter regimes and report what changed.
  • Review support. Use receipts and settings as evidence during discussion or reassessment.
Fast start

Use the landing page as the briefing layer

Start from the lighter homepage, then send students into one focused lab rather than dropping them into a heavy surface immediately.

Best first labs

Drag and fire are especially classroom-friendly

They communicate the governing idea quickly and reward comparison between scenarios.

Assessment path

Receipts beat screenshots

A saved run is a stronger starting point for moderation or discussion than a static image without context.
